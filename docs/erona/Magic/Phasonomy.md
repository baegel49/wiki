*A note from the Seventh Sanctum's comparative catalogue of magical traditions, compiled in the 137th year of the Cenerian Age.*

Phasonomy is the oldest magical tradition native to the [[Mycean]] and [[Cenerian]] cultures, though we must be precise here, because it is not the oldest tradition we are aware of overall. That distinction belongs to [[Geomancery]], which predates Phasonomy by an unknown but significant age, likely even to the Age of Titans. Phasonomy, on the other hand, was developed some time before the [[Mycean]] Age, when Glimstone was first cut into specific shapes. The [[Sect of Illumination]] is the only organisation known to the Sanctum that is able to wield this power, though we have had some success in our own experiments too. 

Phasonomy is nearly unthinkable without Glimstone. It requires precise cutting, shaping, and wielding in order to achieve anything close to what we might describe as 'magic'. Different levels of precision can achieve different performances: 

A basic level of Phasonomy will allow the wielder to cut a stone that can store and emit light, a relatively simple technique but unattainable without the right know-how. The Luminari use this in everything from walking staves to interior lighting. 

At a more advanced level, Phasonomy allows the wielder to channel stored light into more and more destructive beams, often achieved with lenses of Glimstones to achieve even higher concentrations of light. An advanced user can even achieve powerful fireballs and explosions in this manner. 

At the highest levels, a wielder of Phasonomy can be expected to briefly turn night to day, and to channel stored light within Glimstone in far away places. This requires extreme geometries of Glimstone that has not yet been reproduced at the Sanctum. It is further unclear to us how light from beyond a wielder's own region is channelled, though we do know that there is *some* delay at far enough distances.
