*The following is drawn from "On the Nature and Degrees of Arcanum," a foundational text of the Seventh Sanctum, revised in its current form in the 143rd year of the Cenerian Age.*

Arcanum is the written language shared by the [[Mycean]] and [[Cenerian]] peoples, and is, to the best of our knowledge, the most powerful instrument of magic available to mortal hands. It is the position of the [[Seventh Sanctum]] that Arcanum does not merely describe reality but, under sufficient conditions, actively shapes it by working through the medium of [[Echoes]] to impose the written word upon the world.

We do not use the word "magic" lightly. The Sanctum has spent centuries cataloguing the precise conditions under which written Arcanum produces measurable effects, and the results are unambiguous. What follows is the framework our predecessors developed to describe Arcanum's five ascending degrees of power. It remains the most useful taxonomy we have, though we acknowledge that we can only say *what* each degree does far more confidently than we can say *why*.

#### The Five Degrees

The first degree is the *recording of the already-true* such as a ledger of grain in the granary, a deed for land in lawful possession, or a merchant's stock. At this degree, Arcanum exerts no real force and is merely a precise language. 

The second degree, *witnessing the agreed*, is the first point at which Arcanum begins to act upon the world: a contract between consenting parties, a judgment where facts are not in dispute. Here Arcanum does not create truth so much as fix it, making what was agreed difficult to unmake.

The third degree is *imposing the disputed* and is where the more cautious among us counsel restraint. A binding placed upon someone who has not consented, a judgment rendered over protest are where Arcanum exerts magical force against mortal resistance. Its effects have been documented extensively. 

The fourth degree, *establishing the new*, has been achieved by untrained hands only a handful of times in recorded history. A law with no prior existence, a boundary with no prior agreement, a truth the world had not previously acknowledged are possible but require an exceptional writer, exceptional conditions, and, we suspect, something we do not yet understand about the relationship between the writer and their [[Echoes|Echo]]. This degree is what the Sanctum hopes to achieve in all its aspirants. 

The fifth degree — *altering the world itself* — is extreme. Moving mountains. Redirecting rivers. Raising the dead. The Sanctum itself has only produced one known candidate of this nature who did not go insane, Azuth himself. All others that were known were outcast or executed for their insanity. 

#### On the Sect of Pyos

The [[Sect of Pyos]] holds that Arcanum was given to humanity by [[Pyos]] himself at the founding of [[Mycea]], and that it is therefore sacred as the very voice of the god, which is why oaths sworn in Arcanum carry such weight. The Sanctum neither endorses nor refutes this claim. What we observe is that Arcanum functions consistently regardless of whether the writer holds any particular religious view, which suggests its power is a property of the language itself rather than divine favour. We leave the theological question to others.

#### On the Sect of Illumination

The [[Sect of Illumination]] mistrusts Arcanum and written language in general, preferring to preserve knowledge orally and through song. We note that oral traditions are considerably more vulnerable to drift, misremembering, and deliberate alteration than written ones, and that the Sanctum's archives have preserved knowledge the Illumination's oral tradition has long since lost.
