#### Meta
*A wolf-horse-like beast that inhabits the [[Karrow]]. The [[Rurii]] tame these beasts as mounts and as hunting companions.* 



_From the Seventh Sanctum's catalogue of the beasts of the eastern wastes, compiled in the 141st year of the Cenerian Age. Where the Sanctum has not seen a thing with its own eyes, it has set down what is reported, and named the reporting for what it is._

The beast that the men of the League call the karrowhound is neither hound nor horse, though it borrows the figure of both, and the elder catalogues are right to warn that the name misleads the unwary. It is a creature of the [[Karrow]], that [[Haze]]-steeped waste east of [[Osera]], and it is found nowhere that the Haze does not lie low upon the ground. The [[Rurii]], who alone have mastered it, do not call it by any name that we can render; the Great Morr of their oral telling is said to have ridden one out of the wastes, and from this all their riding is descended.

## Of its form

In bulk it is as a courser, broad enough across the back to bear a grown rider, yet it carries the long jaw and the lean gait of a wolf, so that those who meet it for the first time take it for a monster stitched of two animals. Its feet are neither the true hoof of the horse nor the soft pad of the hound, but a splayed and horned foot between the two, which serves it equally upon the hard steppe and the scrambling stone of the mesas. Its coat is a grizzled grey that beads the damp of the [[Haze]] and sheds it, and that renders it near invisible in the mist, in the manner of the Horrors, though for the opposite purpose: the Horror hides to wait, the karrowhound hides to run down.

Most striking is that the karrowhound sees little and needs to see less. Its eyes are clouded and small, and the authorities agree it hunts by scent and by the tremor of the ground, reading the passage of prey through the hard earth as a man reads a road by daylight. In this it is fitted to its country, for in the perpetual Haze of the Karrow the eye is the least useful of the senses, and a beast that trusted to sight would starve.

## Of its habits and its taming

It runs in small bonded bands and does not bay as a dog bays, but utters a low call that carries far through the mist. The [[Rurii]] take the young from the den and rear them from the first, and it is reported that a Ruri and his hound are paired for the one life, hunting together and, in the wars of migration, going to battle together. When [[Xeril's conquests]] drove that people west into [[Ceneria]] and [[Zinnabar]], the karrowhounds went with them; and here is a thing worth the traveller's knowing, for it answers an old confusion. The [[Horrors|Horror]], taken from the Haze, wilts and dies for want of the Mist it breathes. The karrowhound does not. It breeds, hunts, and grows fat far from the Karrow, which is proof enough that it is a true beast of flesh and not, whatever some hold, a Horror grown tame.

## Of differing accounts

Some sages will have it that the karrowhound is no more than a Horror that the Rurii have bent to use, and they point to the shared coat and the shared blindness as their warrant. The Sanctum does not hold with this. The Horror breathes the mist and perishes without it; the karrowhound eats flesh and thrives in the green lands; the two breed each after its own kind and never the one into the other. That they wear the same grey is the work of the same country upon two different beasts, as two unrelated men grow brown under the one sun. The Rurii themselves laugh at the question, and say only that the hound was given to their first Great Morr in the wastes, which the Sanctum records as their telling and not as its own.

## The lesson of the karrowhound

And here the older catalogues append a gloss, which we keep for its use to the preacher. The karrowhound goes near blind through a country where all is hidden, and yet it never loses the trail, for it does not trust the eye but the surer senses beneath. So too, they say, is the faithful soul that walks through a clouded world: it is not the one who sees furthest that holds the truest course, but the one who keeps the scent of the thing it loves. And let no man despise the beast for its ill-made look, for the creature taken for a monster has proved, in the hand of the Rurii, the most faithful of all companions.
