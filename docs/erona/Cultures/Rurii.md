The Rurii are a people that originate from the [[Karrow]], a land east of [[Osera]] and the wastes that separate it from the [[Unsung Lands]]. After [[Xeril's Conquests]], many permanently migrated into the more fertile regions of [[Ceneria]] and [[Zinnabar]]. They refer to themselves as the Mora-Bantár, or simply Morr, and their leaders as Great Morr. 
#### Appearance
Rurii are considered not only a culture but a race apart from other Oserans. They differ quite significantly from other races, particularly in their backward knees and fur-like covering of their bodies, which are hunched. 
#### Origin
The Rurii keep an oral history of their people which states they are the descendants of the runaway slaves of the [[Titans]]. Whatever their origin, their stay in the Karrow has deeply affected them: they are hairier than most, and are often considered 'beastlike' by man other cultures. 
#### Culture
The Rurii have a deep passion for stories and song, using a variety of sounds, clicks, and throat vibrations to bring their tales to life. While they are considered extremely warlike, their culture also has a deep respect for peace (after violent means have been exhausted to save face). 
They are also renowned beast tamers, having tamed the yak-like [[Bana]] of the Karrow which they inhabit, as well as the wolf-horse-like [[Karrowhound]]. 
#### Beliefs
The Rurii's primary deity is the Sky-Mother, though they also believe in the spirits of all living things. They have many minor gods, most who are not known to outsiders. They also perform sacrifices to the [[Titans]], whom they continue to fear. 
#### Relations
Other cultures of [[Osera]] have a dim view of the [[Rurii]]. They often consider them 'beastly', both in appearance and manners. They are, however, respected for their prowess and survivability, which are undeniable to even the staunchest of detractors.
